About

Hello, I'm redshiftzero. I'm an engineer, researcher and cypherpunk. I'm currently the CTO at Freedom of the Press Foundation, where I oversee the organization’s engineering teams.

I sometimes do consulting, most recently for clients including Lockdown Systems (which I'm now a member of) on Cyd, Radiant Commons on their protocol guides, and Lita Foundation on their zkVM Valida.

Previous affiliations:

  • I was a founding engineer working on the cryptography stack at Penumbra Labs, a privacy-preserving decentralized exchange and L1 in the Cosmos ecosystem.
  • I was the lead developer of the SecureDrop whistleblower platform.
  • I was a cofounder and board member of Lucy Parsons Labs, a transparency non-profit based in Chicago.
  • I was a core contributor to the Tor Project, a non-profit that creates the Tor anonymity network and Tor Browser.
  • I was an advisory board member of Distributed Denial of Secrets, a non-profit that archives and publishes hacked and leaked data in the public interest.
  • I was a postdoc researcher at the Center for Data Science and Public Policy (now at Carnegie Mellon).
  • I completed my Ph.D. in Astrophysics in 2015 at the University of Chicago's Kavli Institute of Cosmological Physics.

Previous Projects

Cryptography/Privacy

  • decaf377: An elliptic-curve library in Rust - a prime-order group designed for use in SNARKs over BLS12-377. Work done while at Penumbra Labs.
  • poseidon377: a suite of Rust crates for the Poseidon hash function. Work done while at Penumbra Labs.
  • SecureDrop: A tool for news organizations to communicate with sources. The full list of organizations using SecureDrop can be seen in the directory here.
  • Cyd: A tool to download your data from X, back it up, delete it from the platform, and migrate it to Bluesky.

Civic Technology

  • ICE Detention Map: An open-source dashboard and map showing information about ICE detention facilities.
  • OpenOversight: A tool for police oversight in use on the Burlington VT, Chicago, Oakland, Berkeley, and UC Berkeley Police Departments, as well as in Baltimore. Media: Citylab, Chicagoist, Washington Times
  • City of Cincinnati building code prediction (during 2015 Data Science for Social Good): blog GitHub repository. Media: Wired/Backchannel
  • Data-Driven Early Intervention System for police departments: GitHub repository. Now developed by CMU and commercialized by Benchmark Analytics. Media: Mother Jones, FiveThirtyEight, NPR, wsoctv

Astrophysics

  • cosmocalc.rs: A library for computing cosmological distances in Rust.
  • cosmowebapp: A d3.js interactive visualization of cosmological (matter and CMB) power spectra. Joint with Eric Baxter (Hawaii IfA).
